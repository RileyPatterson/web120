AIA

Client's Name, Relationship and Final Decision Maker Kelani (a classmate from my UX class). She will be the one to make the final decisions.
Client's Business (Website) Name Kelani Toms (because it will primarily be a portfolio website, her name is her brand)
New or Redesign Brand New
Type of Website Primarily a portfolio to showcase her educational and professional work.
Client's Goals To display her creative process, skill-set, and past professional projects to potential work partners, peers and bosses.
Audience Issues Aproach
Potential Employers: For both full time positions as well as contract projects. Also those in a similar position such as mentors and professors.
  • Desiring proof of her past work experience and skills.
  • Looking to see if her background and creative process/ideology make her a good fit for a particular project/organization/educational program, etc.
  • Hoping to contact a designer for a prospective project.
  • A Portfolio which proves her past work experience in the field and displays her creative process across multiple projects.
  • A blog which further documents her creative ideology and perspective.
  • A contact page where the designer can be contacted with questions and oportunities.
Peers: Past and potential work partners, classmates, and those working toward a career in UX. wishing to learn about the paths others have taken.
  • Looking to learn about the path others have taken to break into the field of UX.
  • Seeking advice or opportunities for collaboration with other UX designers.
  • Insight into further opportunities, inspiration and other resources in the local (and digital) UX communities.
  • A collection of projects/places/items the designer takes inspiration from.
  • A contact page.
  • A 'Resources' page with links to helpful online repositories of inspiration and and recomendations for businesses and schools which could help the artist continue to develop their craft.
The Client:
  • A creative resume that she can provide to prospective employers and collaborators.
  • A collection of her past works she can refference, reflect on, and from which she can take lessons/inspiration for future projects.
  • Somewhere she can document her every day UX experiences and lessons she has learned, those which don't comprise entire start-to-finish projects.
  • A portfolio of her past work in UX to back up her resume.
  • A place to store items which have inspired her, which she might use when looking for inspiration for future projects.
  • A blog, where she can keep a record of her daily experiences with UX.
